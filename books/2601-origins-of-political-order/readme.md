Indeed, the most basic forms of cooperation predate the emergence of human beings by millions of years. Biologists have identified two natural sources of cooperative behavior:
- **KIN SELECTION** 
- **RECIPROCAL ALTRUISM**

**Kin selection** - With regard to the first, the name of the game in biological evolution is not the survival of a given organism but the survival of that organism’s genes. individuals of any sexually reproducing species will behave altruistically toward kin in proportion to the number of genes they share. Parents and children, and full brothers and sisters, share 50 percent of their genes, and so will behave more altruistically toward each other than toward first cousins, who share only 25 percent.

[Altruism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Altruism) - the unselfish concern for the welfare of others, characterized by actions that benefit someone else, often at a personal cost or risk to oneself, without expecting rewards.

**Reciprocal altruism** - The ability to cooperate with genetic strangers is referred to by biologists as reciprocal altruism, and in addition to kin selection is the second major biological source of social behavior found in many species of animals. Social cooperation depends on an individual’s ability to solve what game theorists label **repeated prisoner’s dilemma games**. In these games, individuals potentially benefit by being able to work together, but <u>they can often benefit more if they let other individuals do the cooperating and free-ride off of their efforts. </u>

## 2 Specifically human

Szympansy i ludzie mają 99% shareowanego DNA. Co między innymi distinguishes human beings from their chimplike precedents? Krótka odp: the Language.

Skąd w człowieku wzięły się tak rozwinięte inteligiencja i cognitive skills? W 5 milionów lat, odkąd Homo line broke off from the human-chimp ancestor mózg człowieka potroił się w rozmiarze, co jest giga superszybkim development in evolutionary terms (ewenement na skale zjawiska). Stąd też np kanał rozrodczy kobiety ledwo nadążał za rozmiarem głowy noworodków. 

Where did this cognitive power come from?

Na pierwszy rzut oka może się wydawać, że jest to konieczny krok w adaptacji do fizycznego środowiska, że greater intelligence oferuje przewagę co do polowania, zbieractwa, wytwarzania narzędzi itp. ALE to nie jest satysfakcjonujące wyjaśnienie since many other species also hunt, gather and even use tool WITHOUT having developed anything like human being's coginitive abilities.

Many evolutionary biologists have speculated that the human brain grew as rapidly as it did for a different reason: to be able to cooperate and compete with other human beings. Compete in forms of `CONCLUSIONS.1`.

Im lepiej umiesz interpretować czyjeś zachowanie, tym jesteś lepszy w `CONCLUSIONS.1`, więc przekażesz więcej genów, więc "zostaje" ten skill.

Teoria gier pokazuje, że kooperujemy z tymi co wykazali się to be honest and reliable, a negujemy tych to zachowali się opportunistically.

![](_img/33.png)

Chimpanzees can achieve a band level of social organization of a few dozen individuals because they possess some of the cognitive skills required to solve basic repeated prisoner’s dilemma games. Chimps thus have sufficient memory and communications skills to interpret and predict each other’s likely behavior, out of which evolve leadership and cooperation.

But chimps are unable to move to higher levels of social organization because they do not have **LANGUAGE**. Having language means that knowledge of who was honest and who deceitful no longer depends on direct experience but can be passed on to others as social knowledge.
