# Lexicon Draft — P. Civilizations, Empires, and World Order
(The macro-structures of history)

This section treats civilizations and empires as large-scale systems
that organize territory, populations, resources, and legitimacy
over long time horizons.

---

## Civilization  
**PL:** cywilizacja

**Definition:**  
A large-scale social system characterized by urbanization, complex institutions,
division of labor, and cultural continuity.

**Why it matters:**  
Civilizations define long-term patterns beyond individual states.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civilization

---

## Empire  
**PL:** imperium

**Definition:**  
A political system that exercises authority over diverse territories and populations,
typically beyond its original core.

**Why it matters:**  
Empires are scalable solutions to managing power, surplus, and security.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empire

---

## Core–Periphery Structure  
**PL:** struktura rdzeń–peryferie

**Definition:**  
A system where a central core extracts resources and controls decision-making,
while peripheral regions supply labor, tribute, or raw materials.

**Why it matters:**  
Explains inequality, rebellion, and imperial overstretch.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Core%E2%80%93periphery_model

---

## Province  
**PL:** prowincja

**Definition:**  
An administrative unit governed by the imperial center.

**Why it matters:**  
Provinces are mechanisms for control, taxation, and integration.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Province

---

## Tributary System  
**PL:** system trybutarny

**Definition:**  
A system where subordinate entities provide regular payments or goods
in exchange for protection or recognition.

**Why it matters:**  
Reduces governance costs while maintaining dominance.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tributary_state

---

## Hegemony  
**PL:** hegemonia

**Definition:**  
Dominance exercised through a mix of coercion, incentives, and legitimacy.

**Why it matters:**  
Hegemony stabilizes systems without direct control everywhere.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hegemony

---

## Vassal System  
**PL:** system wasalny

**Definition:**  
A hierarchical relationship where local rulers retain autonomy
in exchange for loyalty and service.

**Why it matters:**  
Delegates control and reduces administrative burden.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vassal

---

## Satrapy  
**PL:** satrapia

**Definition:**  
A provincial governance system used in ancient empires, notably Persia.

**Why it matters:**  
Early model of delegated imperial administration.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Satrap

---

## Fief  
**PL:** lenno

**Definition:**  
Land granted in exchange for service within a feudal system.

**Why it matters:**  
Represents decentralization of power under resource constraints.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fief

---

## Hegemonic Cycle  
**PL:** cykl hegemoniczny

**Definition:**  
A recurring pattern of rise, dominance, decline, and replacement
of leading powers.

**Why it matters:**  
Explains long-term shifts in world order.

**Wikipedia (related):**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Power_transition_theory

---

## Imperial Expansion  
**PL:** ekspansja imperialna

**Definition:**  
The outward growth of control driven by security needs, surplus, or opportunity.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imperialism

---

## Overextension  
**PL:** przeciążenie (imperialne)

**Definition:**  
A condition where expansion exceeds administrative, fiscal, or military capacity.

**Why it matters:**  
A primary cause of imperial collapse.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imperial_overstretch

---

## Fiscal Crisis  
**PL:** kryzys fiskalny

**Definition:**  
The inability of a state to finance its obligations.

**Why it matters:**  
Often precedes institutional breakdown or reform.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fiscal_crisis

---

## Legitimacy Crisis  
**PL:** kryzys legitymizacji

**Definition:**  
A breakdown in belief that authority is rightful or justified.

**Why it matters:**  
Undermines compliance and accelerates fragmentation.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legitimacy_(political)

---

## Imperial Legitimization  
**PL:** legitymizacja imperialna

**Definition:**  
Narratives and doctrines justifying imperial rule.

**Examples:**  
- Roman universalism  
- civilizing missions  

**Wikipedia (related):**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imperialism

---

## Universalism  
**PL:** uniwersalizm

**Definition:**  
The claim that a political or moral order applies universally.

**Why it matters:**  
Universalism legitimizes expansion beyond ethnic or territorial boundaries.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universalism

---

## Mandate of Heaven  
**PL:** mandat nieba

**Definition:**  
A Chinese doctrine legitimizing rule based on moral governance
and conditional divine approval.

**Why it matters:**  
Introduces conditional legitimacy tied to performance.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandate_of_Heaven

---

## State Collapse  
**PL:** upadek państwa

**Definition:**  
The loss of central authority and institutional capacity.

**Why it matters:**  
Collapse reshapes civilizations and resets power structures.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Societal_collapse

---

## Fragmentation  
**PL:** fragmentacja

**Definition:**  
The breakdown of unified authority into competing local powers.

**Why it matters:**  
Often follows imperial overstretch and legitimacy loss.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_fragmentation
