# Lexicon Draft — N. Evolutionary Psychology and Group Dynamics
(The biological engine beneath social systems)

This section covers evolutionary mechanisms shaping human cooperation,
competition, hierarchy, and conflict. These dynamics operate largely below
conscious awareness but strongly constrain institutions and behavior.

---

## Evolutionary Psychology  
**PL:** psychologia ewolucyjna

**Definition:**  
An approach explaining psychological traits as adaptations shaped by natural selection.

**Why it matters:**  
Human behavior reflects ancestral survival problems, not modern environments.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evolutionary_psychology

---

## Kin Selection  
**PL:** dobór krewniaczy

**Definition:**  
A mechanism favoring behaviors that increase the survival of genetic relatives.

**Why it matters:**  
Explains nepotism, clan loyalty, and family-based cooperation.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kin_selection

---

## Reciprocal Altruism  
**PL:** altruizm wzajemny

**Definition:**  
Cooperation based on repeated interactions and expectation of reciprocity.

**Why it matters:**  
Enables cooperation beyond close kin.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reciprocal_altruism

---

## Coalition  
**PL:** koalicja

**Definition:**  
A temporary or permanent alliance formed to increase collective advantage.

**Why it matters:**  
Coalition-building is a core human political behavior.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coalition

---

## Status  
**PL:** status

**Definition:**  
Relative social ranking that determines access to resources and influence.

**Why it matters:**  
Status-seeking drives competition, signaling, and hierarchy formation.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_status

---

## Intra-group Competition  
**PL:** rywalizacja wewnątrzgrupowa

**Definition:**  
Competition among members of the same group for status, resources, or mates.

**Why it matters:**  
Explains factionalism, elite conflict, and political infighting.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intraspecific_competition

---

## Hierarchy  
**PL:** hierarchia

**Definition:**  
An ordered structure of dominance or authority within a group.

**Why it matters:**  
Hierarchies reduce conflict but concentrate power.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hierarchy

---

## Reputation  
**PL:** reputacja

**Definition:**  
Collective assessment of an individual’s past behavior.

**Why it matters:**  
Reputation enables trust and deters defection.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reputation

---

## Trust  
**PL:** zaufanie

**Definition:**  
The expectation that others will act cooperatively.

**Why it matters:**  
Trust lowers transaction costs and enables large-scale cooperation.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trust_(social_science)

---

## Punishment  
**PL:** kara

**Definition:**  
The imposition of costs on defectors or norm violators.

**Why it matters:**  
Punishment stabilizes cooperation even when costly.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Altruistic_punishment

---

## Norm Enforcement  
**PL:** egzekwowanie norm

**Definition:**  
Mechanisms ensuring compliance with group rules.

**Why it matters:**  
Precedes formal law and institutions.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_norm#Enforcement

---

## Intra-group Conflict  
**PL:** konflikt wewnątrzgrupowy

**Definition:**  
Conflict among members of the same group.

**Why it matters:**  
Limits group cohesion and often triggers institutional reforms.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intragroup_conflict

---

## Inter-group Conflict  
**PL:** konflikt międzygrupowy

**Definition:**  
Conflict between distinct groups competing for resources or dominance.

**Why it matters:**  
Drives warfare, ethnogenesis, and group solidarity.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intergroup_conflict

---

## Ultrasociality  
**PL:** ultraspołeczność

**Definition:**  
The capacity of humans to cooperate in very large groups of non-kin.

**Why it matters:**  
Enables states, religions, and complex economies.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultrasociality

---

## Collective Action Problem  
**PL:** problem działania zbiorowego

**Definition:**  
Situations where individuals benefit from cooperation but have incentives to free-ride.

**Why it matters:**  
Explains the need for institutions, norms, and coercion.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Collective_action_problem
