# Lexicon Draft — H. Demography and Mass Society
(When population size becomes a structural force)

This section covers population dynamics as systemic drivers of economic capacity,
state power, social stability, and collapse. Demography is one of the strongest
long-term constraints in history.

---

## Historical Demography  
**PL:** demografia historyczna

**Definition:**  
The study of population size, structure, and change in past societies.

**Why it matters:**  
Population dynamics constrain labor supply, military potential, and economic growth.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Historical_demography

---

## Mortality  
**PL:** śmiertelność

**Definition:**  
The frequency of death within a population.

**Why it matters:**  
High mortality limits population growth and social complexity.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mortality_rate

---

## Fertility  
**PL:** dzietność

**Definition:**  
The average number of children born per individual or per woman.

**Why it matters:**  
Determines long-term population growth or decline.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fertility

---

## Migration  
**PL:** migracje

**Definition:**  
The movement of populations across regions.

**Why it matters:**  
Migration reshapes labor markets, cultures, and political stability.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_migration

---

## Demographic Transition  
**PL:** przejście demograficzne

**Definition:**  
The shift from high birth and death rates to low birth and death rates.

**Why it matters:**  
Explains population explosions followed by stabilization or decline.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demographic_transition

---

## Demographic Dividend  
**PL:** dywidenda demograficzna

**Definition:**  
Economic growth potential resulting from a high proportion of working-age population.

**Why it matters:**  
Can accelerate development if institutions absorb labor productively.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demographic_dividend

---

## Population Aging  
**PL:** starzenie się społeczeństwa

**Definition:**  
An increase in the median age of a population.

**Why it matters:**  
Raises fiscal and social burdens while shrinking labor supply.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Population_ageing

---

## Urbanization  
**PL:** urbanizacja

**Definition:**  
The increasing concentration of population in cities.

**Why it matters:**  
Cities enable productivity, innovation, and control — but also fragility.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urbanization

---

## Megacity  
**PL:** megamiasto

**Definition:**  
A city with a population typically exceeding 10 million.

**Why it matters:**  
Megacities amplify economic output and systemic risk.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megacity

---

## Infrastructure  
**PL:** infrastruktura

**Definition:**  
Physical systems enabling urban and economic life.

**Examples:**  
- roads  
- water supply  
- sanitation  
- energy networks  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infrastructure

---

## Mass Education  
**PL:** edukacja masowa

**Definition:**  
The provision of standardized education to large populations.

**Why it matters:**  
Enables industrial economies and bureaucratic states.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass_education

---

## Literacy  
**PL:** alfabetyzacja

**Definition:**  
The ability to read and write.

**Why it matters:**  
A prerequisite for administration, law, and mass communication.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Literacy

---

## Human Capital  
**PL:** kapitał ludzki

**Definition:**  
The skills, knowledge, and health embodied in a population.

**Why it matters:**  
Determines long-term productivity and innovation capacity.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_capital

---

## Historical Epidemiology  
**PL:** epidemiologia historyczna

**Definition:**  
The study of disease patterns in historical populations.

**Why it matters:**  
Epidemics act as external shocks reshaping demographics, economies, and power structures.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_disease

---

## Pandemic  
**PL:** pandemia

**Definition:**  
A disease outbreak affecting large geographic areas and populations.

**Examples:**  
- Black Death  
- Spanish Flu  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pandemic

---

## Disease as a Systemic Factor  
**PL:** choroby jako czynnik systemowy

**Definition:**  
Diseases viewed not as isolated events, but as structural forces altering historical trajectories.

**Why it matters:**  
They can trigger labor shortages, social unrest, institutional change, or collapse.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_epidemics
