# Lexicon Draft — Q. Operational Conflict Understanding
(A universal framework for analyzing any conflict)

This section provides an operational toolkit for analyzing armed conflicts,
hybrid conflicts, and prolonged geopolitical confrontations. It is designed
to be applied repeatedly, regardless of region or era.

---

## Actor Mapping  
**PL:** mapowanie aktorów

**Definition:**  
The identification of all relevant participants in a conflict,
their roles, relationships, and dependencies.

**Why it matters:**  
Conflicts are rarely binary; ignoring secondary actors leads to false conclusions.

**Wikipedia (related):**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stakeholder_analysis

---

## State Actor  
**PL:** aktor państwowy

**Definition:**  
A sovereign state exercising formal authority over territory and population.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_(polity)

---

## Proxy Actor  
**PL:** aktor zastępczy (proxy)

**Definition:**  
An actor supported by an external power to advance indirect objectives.

**Why it matters:**  
Proxy warfare reduces direct costs while increasing deniability.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proxy_war

---

## Non-State Actor  
**PL:** aktor niepaństwowy

**Definition:**  
An organized group operating independently of formal state authority.

**Examples:**  
- militias  
- insurgent groups  
- corporations (in limited contexts)

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-state_actor

---

## Warlord  
**PL:** watażka

**Definition:**  
A local power holder exercising military and economic control,
often in weak or collapsed states.

**Why it matters:**  
Warlords fragment authority and prolong conflict.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warlord

---

## Strategic Objectives  
**PL:** cele strategiczne

**Definition:**  
The fundamental aims pursued by actors in a conflict.

**Typical categories:**  
- territory  
- regime security or survival  
- access to resources  
- prestige or status  
- ideological goals

**Wikipedia (related):**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strategic_objective

---

## Territorial Control  
**PL:** kontrola terytorium

**Definition:**  
Physical dominance over geographic space.

**Why it matters:**  
Territory enables taxation, recruitment, and legitimacy claims.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Territory_(animal)

---

## Regime Security  
**PL:** bezpieczeństwo reżimu

**Definition:**  
The survival of governing elites and institutional control.

**Why it matters:**  
Regime survival often outweighs national welfare.

**Wikipedia (related):**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regime_security

---

## Resources  
**PL:** zasoby

**Definition:**  
Material assets that finance or motivate conflict.

**Examples:**  
- oil  
- minerals  
- agricultural land  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_resource

---

## Prestige  
**PL:** prestiż

**Definition:**  
Status, recognition, or symbolic power gained through conflict.

**Why it matters:**  
Prestige influences deterrence and alliance behavior.

**Wikipedia (related):**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prestige_(sociology)

---

## Ideology  
**PL:** ideologia

**Definition:**  
A structured belief system guiding political goals and justification.

**Why it matters:**  
Ideology mobilizes support and legitimizes costs.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ideology

---

## Capabilities  
**PL:** zdolności

**Definition:**  
The material and organizational means available to actors.

**Core components:**  
- manpower  
- industrial capacity  
- logistics  
- technology  
- alliances

**Wikipedia (related):**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_capability

---

## Manpower  
**PL:** zasoby ludzkie / siła żywa

**Definition:**  
The quantity and quality of personnel available for conflict.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manpower

---

## Industrial Capacity  
**PL:** potencjał przemysłowy

**Definition:**  
The ability to produce weapons, supplies, and infrastructure.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_economy

---

## Logistics  
**PL:** logistyka

**Definition:**  
The systems enabling sustained operations over time and distance.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_logistics

---

## Alliances  
**PL:** sojusze

**Definition:**  
Formal or informal agreements providing external support.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_alliance

---

## Constraints  
**PL:** ograniczenia

**Definition:**  
Factors limiting the range of viable strategies.

**Typical constraints:**  
- public opinion  
- economic capacity  
- geography  
- demography

**Wikipedia (related):**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constraint_(mathematics)

---

## Public Opinion  
**PL:** opinia publiczna

**Definition:**  
Collective attitudes of the population toward a conflict.

**Why it matters:**  
Limits duration and escalation in mass societies.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_opinion

---

## War Fatigue  
**PL:** zmęczenie wojną

**Definition:**  
Declining public or elite willingness to sustain conflict.

**Wikipedia (related):**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_fatigue

---

## Escalation Spiral  
**PL:** spirala eskalacji

**Definition:**  
A feedback process where actions provoke increasingly severe responses.

**Wikipedia (related):**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Escalation

---

## Window of Opportunity  
**PL:** okno okazji

**Definition:**  
A temporary period where action is unusually advantageous.

**Why it matters:**  
Missed windows often determine long-term outcomes.

**Wikipedia (related):**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Window_of_opportunity

---

## Bargaining Range  
**PL:** przestrzeń negocjacyjna

**Definition:**  
The range of possible agreements preferable to continued conflict.

**Why it matters:**  
If no bargaining range exists, war persists.

**Wikipedia (related):**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bargaining

---

## Mediation  
**PL:** mediacja

**Definition:**  
Third-party intervention aimed at facilitating negotiation.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediation

---

## Ceasefire  
**PL:** rozejm / zawieszenie broni

**Definition:**  
A temporary halt in fighting, formal or informal.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ceasefire

---

## Frozen Conflict  
**PL:** zamrożony konflikt

**Definition:**  
A conflict without active fighting but without political resolution.

**Why it matters:**  
Maintains instability while preventing settlement.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frozen_conflict

---

## Low-Intensity Conflict  
**PL:** konflikt o niskiej intensywności

**Definition:**  
Prolonged conflict below the threshold of conventional war.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low-intensity_conflict
