# Lexicon Draft — I. Religions and Belief Systems
(Structure, doctrine, and institutions)

This section treats religions as structured belief systems that organize meaning,
legitimize authority, and enable large-scale cooperation.

---

## Religion  
**PL:** religia

**Definition:**  
A system of beliefs, practices, and institutions relating humans to transcendent or sacred concepts.

**Why it matters:**  
Religion provides meaning, moral order, and legitimacy beyond material constraints.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religion

---

## Ethnic Religion  
**PL:** religia etniczna

**Definition:**  
A religion closely tied to a specific ethnic group, culture, or territory.

**Why it matters:**  
Limits expansion but strengthens group identity.

**Examples:**  
- ancient Greek religion  
- Shinto (historically)

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnic_religion

---

## Universalist Religion  
**PL:** religia uniwersalistyczna

**Definition:**  
A religion that claims universal applicability and seeks converts.

**Why it matters:**  
Enables large, trans-ethnic communities.

**Examples:**  
- Christianity  
- Islam  
- Buddhism

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universalist_religion

---

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henotheism

---

**Definition:**  
Belief that non-human entities possess spiritual essence.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animism

---

## Shamanism  
**PL:** szamanizm

**Definition:**  
A belief system centered on intermediaries (shamans) who interact with the spirit world.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shamanism

---

## Canon  
**PL:** kanon

**Definition:**  
An authoritative set of texts or doctrines recognized by a religious community.

**Why it matters:**  
Canon stabilizes belief and limits interpretive chaos.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canon_(religion)

---

## Orthodoxy  
**PL:** ortodoksja

**Definition:**  
Correct belief as defined by religious authority.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orthodoxy

---

## Orthopraxy  
**PL:** ortopraksja

**Definition:**  
Correct practice emphasized over correct belief.

**Why it matters:**  
Some religions prioritize ritual behavior rather than doctrine.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orthopraxy

---

## Heresy  
**PL:** herezja

**Definition:**  
Beliefs or doctrines that deviate from established orthodoxy.

**Why it matters:**  
Defines boundaries of acceptable belief.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heresy

---

## Schism  
**PL:** schizma

**Definition:**  
A formal split within a religious community.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schism

---

## Reformation  
**PL:** reforma (religijna)

**Definition:**  
A movement seeking structural or doctrinal change within a religion.

**Examples:**  
- Protestant Reformation

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reformation

---

## Priesthood  
**PL:** kapłaństwo

**Definition:**  
A class of individuals authorized to perform religious rituals.

**Why it matters:**  
Creates institutional continuity and authority.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Priesthood

---

## Religious Institutions  
**PL:** instytucje religijne

**Definition:**  
Organized structures managing doctrine, rituals, and resources.

**Examples:**  
- churches  
- monasteries  
- religious courts  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religious_institution

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religious_law

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secularization

---

## Laicization

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laicity

---

## Fundamentalism  
**PL:** fundamentalizm

**Definition:**  
A movement emphasizing strict adherence to perceived original doctrines.

**Why it matters:**  
Often arises in response to modernization or secularization.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fundamentalism

## Religion as a Technology of Cooperation  
**PL:** religia jako „technologia kooperacji”

**Definition:**  
A functional interpretation of religion as a system enabling large-scale trust,
norm enforcement, and cooperation.

**Why it matters:**  
Explains why religions scale societies beyond kinship.

**Wikipedia (related concepts):**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evolutionary_origin_of_religions
