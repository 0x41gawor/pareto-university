# Lexicon Draft — M. Behavioral Economics and Decision Psychology
(How humans actually decide under constraints)

This section covers systematic deviations from rational choice,
showing how cognitive limits, biases, and social context shape decisions
at individual and collective levels.

---

## Bounded Rationality  
**PL:** ograniczona racjonalność

**Definition:**  
The idea that decision-makers operate under cognitive, informational,
and time constraints, preventing full optimization.

**Why it matters:**  
Most real-world decisions are satisficing, not optimizing.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bounded_rationality

---

## Heuristics  
**PL:** heurystyki

**Definition:**  
Mental shortcuts used to make decisions quickly with limited information.

**Why it matters:**  
Heuristics reduce cognitive load but introduce systematic errors.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heuristic

---

## Cognitive Bias  
**PL:** błąd poznawczy (bias)

**Definition:**  
A predictable deviation from rational judgment caused by cognitive limitations.

**Why it matters:**  
Biases are stable and exploitable, not random.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cognitive_bias

---

## Loss Aversion  
**PL:** awersja do straty

**Definition:**  
The tendency to perceive losses as more painful than equivalent gains are pleasurable.

**Why it matters:**  
Explains risk-averse behavior and resistance to change.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loss_aversion

---

## Framing Effect  
**PL:** efekt ramowania

**Definition:**  
Changes in decision outcomes caused by how options are presented.

**Why it matters:**  
Identical choices produce different behavior under different frames.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Framing_effect_(psychology)

---

## Endowment Effect  
**PL:** efekt posiadania

**Definition:**  
The tendency to value owned objects more highly than identical unowned ones.

**Why it matters:**  
Explains market stickiness and resistance to redistribution.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endowment_effect

---

## Hyperbolic Discounting  
**PL:** dyskontowanie hiperboliczne

**Definition:**  
The tendency to prefer immediate rewards disproportionately over future ones.

**Why it matters:**  
Explains procrastination, addiction, and underinvestment.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyperbolic_discounting

---

## Time Preferences  
**PL:** preferencje czasowe

**Definition:**  
The relative valuation of present versus future outcomes.

**Why it matters:**  
Shapes savings, investment, and long-term planning capacity.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_preference

---

## Prospect Theory  
**PL:** teoria perspektywy

**Definition:**  
A theory describing how people evaluate gains and losses asymmetrically.

**Why it matters:**  
Explains deviations from expected utility theory.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prospect_theory

---

## Risk  
**PL:** ryzyko

**Definition:**  
Situations where probabilities of outcomes are known or estimable.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Risk

---

## Uncertainty (Knightian Uncertainty)  
**PL:** niepewność (niepewność Knighta)

**Definition:**  
Situations where probabilities are unknown or unknowable.

**Why it matters:**  
Under uncertainty, rational calculation breaks down.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knightian_uncertainty

---

## Social Norms  
**PL:** normy społeczne

**Definition:**  
Informal rules governing acceptable behavior within a group.

**Why it matters:**  
Norms coordinate behavior without formal enforcement.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_norm

---

## Conformism  
**PL:** konformizm

**Definition:**  
The tendency to align behavior or beliefs with group expectations.

**Why it matters:**  
Explains rapid opinion shifts and mass behavior.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conformity

---

## Social Identity Theory  
**PL:** teoria tożsamości społecznej

**Definition:**  
A theory explaining how group membership shapes self-perception and behavior.

**Why it matters:**  
Identity can override material incentives.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_identity_theory
