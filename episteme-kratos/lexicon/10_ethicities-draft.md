# Lexicon Draft — J. Ethnicity, Nation, and Culture
(Identity as a structural force)

This section covers ethnicity, language, and cultural identity as mechanisms
of group formation, boundary maintenance, and political mobilization.

---

## Ethnos  
**PL:** etnos

**Definition:**  
A group of people sharing a perceived common origin, culture, language, or ancestry.

**Why it matters:**  
Ethnos precedes the modern nation and often exists without a state.

**Examples:**  
- ancient Greek ethnē  
- tribal or clan-based groups  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnic_group

---

## Nation  
**PL:** naród

**Definition:**  
A socially constructed community bound by shared identity, culture, history,
and often language.

**Why it matters:**  
Nations provide emotional cohesion and political legitimacy.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nation

---

## Nationalism  
**PL:** nacjonalizm

**Definition:**  
An ideology asserting that the nation should be the primary unit of political organization.

**Why it matters:**  
Nationalism mobilizes populations and reshapes political borders.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nationalism

---

## Ethnonationalism  
**PL:** etnonacjonalizm

**Definition:**  
A form of nationalism defining the nation primarily through ethnicity.

**Why it matters:**  
Creates strong in-group cohesion but increases exclusion and conflict risk.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnic_nationalism

---

## Nation-State  
**PL:** państwo narodowe

**Definition:**  
A political entity where state authority aligns with a national identity.

**Why it matters:**  
Becomes dominant in the modern era, especially after the 19th century.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nation_state

---

## Language as a Group Marker  
**PL:** język jako marker grupy

**Definition:**  
Language as a primary signal of group membership and identity.

**Why it matters:**  
Language enables boundary maintenance and collective memory.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Language_and_identity

---

## Dialect Continuum  
**PL:** kontinuum dialektalne

**Definition:**  
A range of dialects where neighboring varieties are mutually intelligible,
but distant ones are not.

**Why it matters:**  
Shows that linguistic boundaries are gradual, not discrete.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dialect_continuum

---

## Liturgical Language  
**PL:** język liturgiczny

**Definition:**  
A language reserved for religious or ritual use.

**Why it matters:**  
Preserves tradition and reinforces authority.

**Examples:**  
- Latin in Catholicism  
- Classical Arabic in Islam  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liturgical_language

---

## Diaspora  
**PL:** diaspora

**Definition:**  
A population dispersed from an original homeland but maintaining a shared identity.

**Why it matters:**  
Diasporas influence politics, economics, and cultural transmission.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diaspora

---

## Minority  
**PL:** mniejszość (etniczna / kulturowa)

**Definition:**  
A group differing from the dominant population in ethnicity, language, or culture.

**Why it matters:**  
Minorities often become focal points of political tension.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minority_group

---

## Ethnopolitical Conflict  
**PL:** konflikt etnopolityczny

**Definition:**  
Conflict driven by ethnic identity combined with political claims.

**Why it matters:**  
Such conflicts are often persistent and difficult to resolve.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnic_conflict

---

## Ethnic Boundaries  
**PL:** granice etniczne

**Definition:**  
Socially maintained distinctions between groups, independent of cultural content.

**Why it matters:**  
Boundaries persist even as cultures change.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnic_boundary

---

## Boundary Maintenance  
**PL:** utrzymywanie granic (etnicznych)

**Definition:**  
Practices that preserve distinctions between groups.

**Examples:**  
- endogamy  
- language use  
- ritual differences  

**Wikipedia (related):**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnicity

---

## Imagined Communities  
**PL:** wyobrażone wspólnoty

**Definition:**  
Communities constructed through shared narratives rather than direct interaction.

**Why it matters:**  
Explains how large nations sustain cohesion.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imagined_communities

---

## Colonialism  
**PL:** kolonializm

**Definition:**  
The control of one territory and population by another, often distant, power.

**Why it matters:**  
Reshapes identities, economies, and borders.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colonialism

---

## Decolonization  
**PL:** dekolonizacja

**Definition:**  
The process by which colonies gain independence.

**Why it matters:**  
Often leaves unresolved ethnic and institutional tensions.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decolonization

---

## Postcolonialism  
**PL:** postkolonializm

**Definition:**  
The study of the cultural, political, and economic legacy of colonial rule.

**Why it matters:**  
Explains persistent inequalities and identity conflicts.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Postcolonialism
