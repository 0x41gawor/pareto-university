# Lexicon Draft — G. Geopolitics and International Relations
(Going deeper into the logic of the global system)

This section describes how states interact within the international system,
how power is structured globally, and how constraints translate into strategy.

---

## International System  
**PL:** system międzynarodowy

**Definition:**  
The overall structure formed by interactions among states and other major actors.

**Why it matters:**  
The system constrains what strategies are viable for individual states.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_system

---

## Unipolar System  
**PL:** system jednobiegunowy

**Definition:**  
A system dominated by a single superpower.

**Examples:**  
- United States after the Cold War

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unipolarity

---

## Bipolar System  
**PL:** system dwubiegunowy

**Definition:**  
A system dominated by two competing superpowers.

**Examples:**  
- United States vs Soviet Union (Cold War)

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bipolarity

---

## Multipolar System  
**PL:** system wielobiegunowy

**Definition:**  
A system with multiple centers of power.

**Examples:**  
- Europe before World War I  
- contemporary global system (arguably)

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multipolarity

---

## Realism (International Relations)  
**PL:** realizm (stosunki międzynarodowe)

**Definition:**  
A theoretical framework viewing states as rational actors pursuing power and security.

**Core assumptions:**  
- anarchy of the international system  
- primacy of survival and power  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Realism_(international_relations)

---

## Liberalism (International Relations)  
**PL:** liberalizm (stosunki międzynarodowe)

**Definition:**

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberalism_(international_relations)
