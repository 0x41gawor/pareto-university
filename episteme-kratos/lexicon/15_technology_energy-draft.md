# Lexicon Draft — O. Technology and Energy as Meta-Factors
(The ceiling of what civilizations can do)

This section treats energy and technology as meta-constraints that define
the upper limits of productivity, military power, institutional complexity,
and population size.

---

## Energy Base  
**PL:** baza energetyczna

**Definition:**  
The dominant sources of energy used by a society to perform work.

**Typical sequence (historical):**  
- biomass (human and animal muscle, wood)  
- coal  
- oil and gas  
- electrification  
- nuclear energy / renewables (RES)

**Why it matters:**  
Energy density and scalability determine the size and complexity of civilizations.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy_transition

---

## Biomass Energy  
**PL:** energia biomasy

**Definition:**  
Energy derived from organic materials, including food, wood, and animal labor.

**Why it matters:**  
Limits societies to low productivity and slow growth.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biomass

---

## Fossil Fuels  
**PL:** paliwa kopalne

**Definition:**  
Energy sources formed from ancient organic matter (coal, oil, natural gas).

**Why it matters:**  
Enable industrialization, mass transport, and modern warfare.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fossil_fuel

---

## Electrification  
**PL:** elektryfikacja

**Definition:**  
The widespread adoption of electricity as a general-purpose energy carrier.

**Why it matters:**  
Decouples energy production from energy use, massively increasing flexibility.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrification

---

## Nuclear Energy  
**PL:** energia jądrowa

**Definition:**  
Energy derived from nuclear reactions.

**Why it matters:**  
Extremely high energy density with geopolitical and safety implications.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuclear_power

---

## Renewable Energy Sources (RES)  
**PL:** odnawialne źródła energii (OZE)

**Definition:**  
Energy sources replenished naturally, such as solar, wind, and hydro.

**Why it matters:**  
Reshapes energy geopolitics and infrastructure requirements.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renewable_energy

---

## Technological Revolution  
**PL:** rewolucja technologiczna

**Definition:**  
A period of rapid, systemic technological change affecting multiple sectors.

**Examples:**  
- Neolithic Revolution  
- Industrial Revolution  
- Digital Revolution  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technological_revolution

---

## Technology Diffusion  
**PL:** dyfuzja technologii

**Definition:**  
The process by which technologies spread across societies and regions.

**Why it matters:**  
Early adopters gain temporary advantages; diffusion erodes them.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diffusion_of_innovations

---

## Technological Comparative Advantage  
**PL:** przewaga komparatywna technologiczna

**Definition:**  
An advantage arising from superior technology or technical know-how.

**Why it matters:**  
Shapes trade patterns, military dominance, and economic hierarchies.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparative_advantage

---

## Dual-Use Technology  
**PL:** technologie podwójnego zastosowania

**Definition:**  
Technologies with both civilian and military applications.

**Examples:**  
- nuclear technology  
- aerospace  
- AI  

**Why it matters:**  
Blurs the line between economic development and military power.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dual-use_technology

---

## Critical Infrastructure  
**PL:** infrastruktura krytyczna

**Definition:**  
Systems essential for the functioning of society and the economy.

**Examples:**  
- energy grids  
- transport networks  
- communications  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Critical_infrastructure

---

## Industrial Base  
**PL:** baza przemysłowa

**Definition:**  
The capacity of a society to produce goods, especially at scale.

**Why it matters:**  
Determines wartime production, resilience, and strategic autonomy.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Industrial_base

---

## Total Factor Productivity (TFP)  
**PL:** produktywność całkowita czynników (TFP – intuicyjnie)

**Definition:**  
A measure of how efficiently labor and capital are used to produce output.

**Intuition:**  
TFP captures technology, organization, know-how, and institutional quality.

**Why it matters:**  
Explains growth beyond simple accumulation of resources.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Total_factor_productivity
