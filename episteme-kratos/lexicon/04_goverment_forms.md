# Lexicon Draft — D. Forms of Government and Political Systems
(Monarchy vs Republic vs Democracy, and beyond)

This section describes different ways political power is structured, distributed,
and exercised within states. These are *forms of rule*, not moral evaluations.

---

## Monarchy  
**PL:** monarchia

**Definition:**  
A system of government where supreme authority is held by a single ruler (monarch).

**Core feature:**  
Personalized rule, often legitimized by tradition or religion.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monarchy

---

## Hereditary Monarchy  
**PL:** monarchia dziedziczna

**Definition:**  
Monarchical power passed through family lineage.

**Why it matters:**  
Provides continuity but risks incompetent succession.

**Examples:**  
- medieval European kingdoms  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hereditary_monarchy

---

## Elective Monarchy  
**PL:** monarchia elekcyjna

**Definition:**  
A monarchy where the ruler is chosen by a select group.

**Examples:**  
- Holy Roman Empire  
- Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elective_monarchy

---

## Absolute Monarchy  
**PL:** monarchia absolutna

**Definition:**  
A monarchy with no formal legal limits on the ruler’s power.

**Why it matters:**  
Maximizes centralization but increases legitimacy risk.

**Examples:**  
- France under Louis XIV  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Absolute_monarchy

---

## Constitutional Monarchy  
**PL:** monarchia konstytucyjna

**Definition:**  
A monarchy constrained by a constitution or legal framework.

**Why it matters:**  
Separates symbolic authority from governance.

**Examples:**  
- United Kingdom  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitutional_monarchy

---

## Republic  
**PL:** republika

**Definition:**  
A system where political authority is derived from a body of citizens rather than a monarch.

**Core feature:**  
Rule is exercised through institutions, not personal sovereignty.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic

---

## Oligarchic Republic  
**PL:** republika oligarchiczna

**Definition:**  
A republic where power is held by a small elite group.

**Examples:**  
- Venetian Republic  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oligarchy

---

## Aristocratic Republic  
**PL:** republika arystokratyczna

**Definition:**  
A republic governed by a hereditary or socially defined elite.

**Examples:**  
- Roman Republic (early/mid period)

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aristocracy

---

## Mixed Republic  
**PL:** republika mieszana

**Definition:**  
A system combining elements of monarchy, aristocracy, and democracy.

**Why it matters:**  
Designed to balance interests and prevent dominance by one group.

**Examples:**  
- Roman Republic (theoretical model)  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mixed_government

---

## Democracy  
**PL:** demokracja

**Definition:**  
A system where political authority is derived from the people.

**Core tension:**  
Participation vs scalability.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democracy

---

## Direct Democracy  
**PL:** demokracja bezpośrednia

**Definition:**  
Citizens directly decide on policies and laws.

**Limitations:**  
Works only at small scales.

**Examples:**  
- Ancient Athens  
- Swiss referenda (partially)

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Direct_democracy

---

## Representative Democracy  
**PL:** demokracja przedstawicielska

**Definition:**  
Citizens elect representatives to govern on their behalf.

**Why it matters:**  
Enables democracy at large scale.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Representative_democracy

---

## Liberal Democracy  
**PL:** demokracja liberalna

**Definition:**  
Democracy combined with rule of law and protection of individual rights.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberal_democracy

---

## Illiberal Democracy  
**PL:** demokracja nieliberalna

**Definition:**  
A system with elections but weak protections for rights and institutions.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Illiberal_democracy

---

## Authoritarianism  
**PL:** autorytaryzm

**Definition:**  
A system where power is concentrated and political pluralism is limited.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Authoritarianism

---

## Totalitarianism  
**PL:** totalitaryzm

**Definition:**  
A regime seeking total control over society, including ideology and private life.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Totalitarianism

---

## Fascism  
**PL:** faszyzm

**Definition:**  
An authoritarian ideology emphasizing nationalism, hierarchy, and mobilization.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fascism

---

## Communism  
**PL:** komunizm

**Definition:**  
An ideology aiming at a classless society through collective ownership.

**Note:**  
Here treated as a **regime type**, not an economic theory.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communism

---

## Theocracy  
**PL:** teokracja

**Definition:**  
A system where political authority is derived from religious doctrine.

**Examples:**  
- Papal States  
- modern Iran  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theocracy

---

## Empire  
**PL:** cesarstwo / imperium

**Definition:**  
A political entity ruling over diverse territories and peoples.

**Core feature:**  
Asymmetry between center and periphery.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empire

---

## Sultanate  
**PL:** sułtanat

**Definition:**  
A state ruled by a sultan, typically within Islamic political tradition.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sultanate

---

## Caliphate  
**PL:** kalifat

**Definition:**  
An Islamic polity led by a caliph, combining religious and political authority.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caliphate

---

## Emirate  
**PL:** emirat

**Definition:**  
A state ruled by an emir.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emirate

---

## Federation  
**PL:** federacja

**Definition:**  
A political system where sovereignty is shared between central and regional governments.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federation

---

## Confederation  
**PL:** konfederacja

**Definition:**  
A loose association of sovereign entities.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confederation

---

## Unitary State  
**PL:** państwo unitarne

**Definition:**  
A state where sovereignty is concentrated at the center.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unitary_state

---

## Parliamentarism  
**PL:** parlamentaryzm

**Definition:**  
A system where the executive derives authority from the legislature.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliamentary_system

---

## Presidentialism  
**PL:** prezydencjalizm

**Definition:**  
A system with a strong, directly elected executive.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presidential_system

---

## Semi-Presidentialism  
**PL:** system semiprezydencki

**Definition:**  
A hybrid system combining presidential and parliamentary elements.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semi-presidential_system

---

## Separation of Powers  
**PL:** trójpodział władzy

**Definition:**  
The division of government into legislative, executive, and judicial branches.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Separation_of_powers

---

## Constitutionalism  
**PL:** konstytucjonalizm

**Definition:**  
The principle that government power must be constrained by law.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitutionalism

---

## Checks and Balances  
**PL:** mechanizmy równowagi i kontroli

**Definition:**  
Institutional mechanisms preventing concentration of power.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Checks_and_balances

---

## Civil Rights  
**PL:** prawa obywatelskie

**Definition:**  
Rights protecting individuals from arbitrary state action.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civil_rights

---

## Citizenship  
**PL:** obywatelstwo

**Definition:**  
Formal membership in a political community.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Citizenship

---

## Sovereignty  
**PL:** suwerenność

**Definition:**  
Supreme authority within a territory.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereignty

---

## Jurisdiction  
**PL:** jurysdykcja

**Definition:**  
The legal authority to govern or adjudicate within a domain.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jurisdiction
