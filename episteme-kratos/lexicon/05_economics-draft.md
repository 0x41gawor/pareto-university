# Lexicon Draft — E. Economy and the “Engine of Civilization”

This section describes economic mechanisms as the underlying engine of civilizational
development. These concepts explain how resources are produced, distributed, captured,
and transformed into power, institutions, and conflict.

---

## Mode of Production  
**PL:** tryb produkcji

**Definition:**  
A dominant way in which a society organizes the production of goods and services.

**Why it matters:**  
The mode of production sets hard constraints on social structure, institutions, and scale.

**Main types (broad sense):**  
- hunter-gatherer  
- agrarian  
- industrial  
- post-industrial  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mode_of_production

---

## Hunter-Gatherer Economy  
**PL:** gospodarka łowiecko-zbieracka

**Definition:**  
An economic system based on hunting animals and gathering wild plants.

**Key characteristics:**  
- low surplus  
- high mobility  
- minimal specialization  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hunter-gatherer

---

## Agrarian Economy  
**PL:** gospodarka agrarna

**Definition:**  
An economy centered on agriculture and land cultivation.

**Why it matters:**  
Enables surplus, population growth, and state formation.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agrarian_society

---

## Industrial Economy  
**PL:** gospodarka industrialna

**Definition:**  
An economy based on mechanized production and fossil energy.

**Why it matters:**  
Massively increases productivity and scale.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Industrial_society

---

## Post-Industrial Economy  
**PL:** gospodarka postindustrialna

**Definition:**  
An economy dominated by services, information, and knowledge.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Post-industrial_society

---

## Productivity  
**PL:** produktywność

**Definition:**  
The amount of output produced per unit of input (labor, land, capital).

**Why it matters:**  
Higher productivity enables surplus and specialization.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Productivity

---

## Economic Surplus  
**PL:** nadwyżka ekonomiczna

**Definition:**  
Production beyond what is required for immediate subsistence.

**Why it matters:**  
Surplus funds elites, states, armies, and culture.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_surplus

---

Increases efficiency and interdependence.

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Division_of_labour

## Specialization
