# Lexicon Draft — K. Law and Social Order
(When power is formalized and stabilized)

This section treats law as a system for formalizing authority, stabilizing expectations,
and managing conflict within and between political entities.

---

## Constitution  
**PL:** konstytucja

**Definition:**  
The fundamental legal framework defining the structure of the state,
distribution of powers, and basic rights.

**Why it matters:**  
A constitution limits arbitrariness and stabilizes political order.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution

---

## Public Law  
**PL:** prawo publiczne

**Definition:**  
Law governing relationships between individuals and the state.

**Examples:**  
- constitutional law  
- administrative law  
- criminal law  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_law

---

## Private Law  
**PL:** prawo prywatne

**Definition:**  
Law governing relationships between private individuals or entities.

**Examples:**  
- contract law  
- property law  
- family law  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Private_law

---

## Human Rights  
**PL:** prawa człowieka

**Definition:**  
Rights claimed to belong to all humans by virtue of being human.

**Why it matters:**  
They function as moral and legal constraints on state power.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_rights

---

## International Law  
**PL:** prawo międzynarodowe

**Definition:**  
A body of rules governing relations between states and other international actors.

**Why it matters:**  
Provides predictability in an anarchic international system.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_law

---

## Law of War  
**PL:** prawo wojny

**Definition:**  
Legal norms regulating the use of force and conduct during armed conflict.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Law_of_war

---

## *Ius ad Bellum*  
**PL:** prawo do wojny

**Definition:**  
Rules governing when a state may lawfully resort to armed force.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jus_ad_bellum

---

## *Ius in Bello*  
**PL:** prawo wojenne (w trakcie konfliktu)

**Definition:**  
Rules governing conduct during warfare, regardless of the legality of the war itself.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_humanitarian_law

---

## Treaty  
**PL:** traktat

**Definition:**  
A formal, legally binding agreement between states.

**Why it matters:**  
Treaties create predictable commitments across borders.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty

---

## Convention  
**PL:** konwencja

**Definition:**  
A multilateral treaty establishing general norms or standards.

**Examples:**  
- Geneva Conventions  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convention_(treaty)

---

## Recognition of a State  
**PL:** uznanie państwa

**Definition:**  
The act by which existing states acknowledge a new entity as a sovereign state.

**Why it matters:**  
Recognition enables participation in international relations.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recognition_(international_law)

---

## Sovereignty  
**PL:** suwerenność

**Definition:**  
Supreme authority within a territory, free from external control.

**Why it matters:**  
Sovereignty is the foundational concept of the modern state system.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereignty

---

## Judicial System  
**PL:** system sądownictwa

**Definition:**  
Institutions responsible for interpreting and enforcing the law.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judiciary

---

## Precedent  
**PL:** precedens

**Definition:**  
A legal decision that serves as an authoritative example in future cases.

**Why it matters:**  
Precedent ensures consistency and predictability.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Precedent

---

## Codification  
**PL:** kodyfikacja

**Definition:**  
The systematic collection and organization of laws into codes.

**Why it matters:**  
Reduces ambiguity and centralizes legal authority.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Codification_(law)

---

## Legitimacy  
**PL:** legitymacja

**Definition:**  
The perception that authority is justified and rightful.

**Why it matters:**  
Legitimacy reduces the need for coercion.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legitimacy_(political)

---

## Legality  
**PL:** legalność

**Definition:**  
Conformity with established law.

**Why it matters:**  
Actions may be legal without being legitimate.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legality

---

## Validity (Legal Validity)  
**PL:** prawomocność / ważność prawna

**Definition:**  
The formal binding force of a legal act within a legal system.

**Why it matters:**  
Distinguishes enforceable law from mere declaration.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Validity_(law)
