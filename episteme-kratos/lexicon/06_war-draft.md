# Lexicon Draft — F. War, Violence, and Security
(Why power ultimately rests on coercion)

This section covers war, violence, and security as systemic phenomena.
These concepts explain how political orders defend themselves, expand,
or collapse under pressure.

---

## War  
**PL:** wojna

**Definition:**  
Organized, large-scale violence between political entities to achieve strategic objectives.

**Why it matters:**  
War is not a breakdown of order, but often a continuation of political coordination by other means.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War

---

## Total War  
**PL:** wojna totalna

**Definition:**  
A form of warfare where entire societies are mobilized and civilian–military boundaries collapse.

**Examples:**  
- World War I  
- World War II  

**Why it matters:**  
Transforms states, economies, and social structures.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Total_war

---

## Limited War  
**PL:** wojna ograniczona

**Definition:**  
A conflict with restricted objectives, means, or geographic scope.

**Why it matters:**  
Most wars in history are limited due to cost constraints.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Limited_war

---

## Positional Warfare  
**PL:** wojna pozycyjna

**Definition:**  
Warfare focused on holding territory rather than maneuver.

**Examples:**  
- trench warfare in WWI  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trench_warfare

---

## Maneuver Warfare  
**PL:** wojna manewrowa

**Definition:**  
Warfare emphasizing movement, speed, and disruption.

**Examples:**  
- Blitzkrieg  

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maneuver_warfare

---

## Mobilization  
**PL:** mobilizacja

**Definition:**  
The process of preparing and organizing resources for war.

**Why it matters:**  
Determines a society’s ability to sustain conflict.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mobilization

---

## Logistics  
**PL:** logistyka

**Definition:**  
The planning and execution of supply, transport, and maintenance.

**Why it matters:**  
Logistics often determine victory more than tactics.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_logistics

---

## Supply Lines  
**PL:** linie zaopatrzenia

**Definition:**  
Routes through which troops receive supplies.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Line_of_communication

---

## Power Projection  
**PL:** projekcja siły

**Definition:**  
The ability to deploy military force beyond one’s borders.

**Why it matters:**  
Defines global and regional influence.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Power_projection

**PL:** rewolucja militarna

**Definition:**  
A period of rapid change in warfare driven by technology and organization.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_Revolution

## Nuclear Weapons  
**PL:** broń jądrowa

**Definition:**  
Weapons deriving destructive power from nuclear reactions.

**Wikipedia:**

## Second-Strike Capability  
**PL:** zdolność drugiego uderzenia

**Definition:**  
The ability to retaliate after absorbing a nuclear attack.

**Why it matters:**  
Makes nuclear war irrational at scale.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_strike

---

## Mutually Assured Destruction (MAD)  
**PL:** wzajemnie zagwarantowane zniszczenie

**Definition:**  
A strategic condition where full-scale nuclear war ensures destruction of all sides.

**Wikipedia:**  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mutual_assured_destruction
